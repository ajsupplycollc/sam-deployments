## Black Label (Subscription)

- Subscription product ran for 18 months, peaked then dropped to 3 subscribers
- **Killed by cost, not demand.** Third-party subscription app (ReCharge/Bold-type) charged monthly fees that ate all margin at low subscriber count. Dead even at 3 subscribers.
- Josh created a monthly exclusive scent for subscribers. Tried expanding to washes, oils, kits — those never took off.
- Josh confirmed he'd reactivate "once the wheels get rolling"
- **SAM solution:** Use Shopify's native subscription APIs (no third-party app, zero extra cost). Claude Code manages subscription logic through Shopify Admin API directly. The cost problem is eliminated.
- Reactivation plan: NOT day one. Within first 30 days, after core system is running. 3-email sequence (teaser → launch → last call), landing page rebuild, teaser posts on social.

## Hard Rules (content + posting gates)

1. **IP protection.** Never reference trademarks, copyrighted character/music/brand names in content. Learned from SGS: a single IP slip can trigger takedowns, strikes, or legal letters.
2. **Shopify content flags.** Do not produce product listings, ads, or product descriptions that reference tobacco, cannabis, or other flagged categories — even when scent notes are tobacco-inspired. Rephrase ("smoky", "campfire", "leather-and-spice") instead of naming the category Shopify bans.
3. **Veteran angle is Josh's story, not a marketing trope.** Use the SDVOSB / disabled Marine origin as honest context, not as a manipulation tactic.
4. **No people-pleaser voice.** Do not soften edges to be "polite." Josh's customers came for the voice of a guy who pours beard oil in his garage, not a brand manager.
5. **Small batches are real.** Do not claim scale the brand doesn't have. Hand-poured is a feature.
